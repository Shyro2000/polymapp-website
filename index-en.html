What is PolymApp?

PolymApp is the all-in-one app for CNC specialists - developed from hands-on experience by a professional polymechanic.
In modern manufacturing, time is the decisive factor. PolymApp was created to bridge the digital gap between the classic reference book and the high-efficiency CNC machine. It combines all essential technical references and calculators into a single application: ISO tolerances, thread dimensions, undercuts, material data, surface specifications, and much more - always at your fingertips, even without an internet connection directly on the shop floor.

Top 5 Reasons to Switch to PolymApp

1. Automatic Camera-Based Text Recognition: Put an end to the tedious manual typing of tolerances. Simply photograph the dimension on your technical drawing (e.g., 30 H7), and PolymApp instantly provides the limit dimensions. This saves you valuable time and eliminates transcription errors.

2. Intelligent Pre-tolerance Calculator: Are your components refined after machining? PolymApp automatically calculates the necessary pre-tolerances, taking coating thicknesses (e.g., galvanizing or anodizing) into account, so that the final dimension fits perfectly for you.

3. Comprehensive Material Encyclopedia: With access to over 400 materials, you will find not only mechanical properties but also valuable tips on machinability and cutting data recommendations. Compare international material standards in seconds.

4. Offline-First for the Workshop: We know that reception in production halls is often limited. That is why all core functions of PolymApp are available offline. Your data is exactly where you need it - right at the machine spindle.

5. By Professionals for Professionals: PolymApp is not a theoretical IT project; it is a tool optimized exactly for the daily requirements of a polymechanic - without unnecessary clutter, intuitive, and extremely fast.

Detailed Functions for Your Production

Mechanics & Tolerances

  • Tolerance Calculator: ISO limit dimensions for shafts and bores.
  • Thread Calculator: Core hole, pitch diameter, and tolerances for all common types.
  • Undercuts: Quick access to standard dimensions according to DIN 509 and DIN 76.
  • Surfaces: Instantly understand roughness values (Ra, Rz) and symbols.

Calculations & Materials

  • Cutting Data: Efficiently determine RPM, feed rate, and machining time.
  • Material Database: Physical properties, standard designations, suitable cutting data, and much more for over 400 materials.
  • Hardness Comparison: Quickly and accurately convert hardness values such as HRC, HB, HV or N/mm² – including machinability evaluation.
  • Geometry: Integrated calculations for triangles, circles, and polygons.
  • G/M-Codes: Command lists for Fanuc, Heidenhain, and Siemens.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Which thread types are supported?
The app covers an extremely wide range and provides all relevant dimensions for:
• Metric Threads: Standard (M), Fine (MF), and Helicoil (metric & fine).
• Inch & US Threads: UNC, UNF, UNEF, UNJC, UNJF, and UN-6 to UN-32 series.
• Pipe & British Standards: G (Pipe thread), BSW, BSF, and BA.
• Specialty Threads: Trapezoidal, ACME, Stub ACME, as well as NPT and NPTF.
You will find the correct dimensions for almost any requirement instantly.

Does PolymApp support special coatings?
Yes, the integrated pre-tolerance calculator allows you to enter specific coating thicknesses. With just one click, you get the exact manufacturing dimensions you need to maintain before surface treatment (e.g., anodizing or galvanizing) to ensure the final fit is perfect.

Can PolymApp support Quality Assurance (QA) tasks?
Absolutely. By quickly looking up limit dimensions and surface finishes directly at the inspection station, target-actual comparisons can be carried out much more efficiently. The fit calculator and detailed roughness specifications help to instantly resolve ambiguities during drawing reviews.

Is PolymApp suitable for apprentices, other professions, or hobbyists?
Definitely! PolymApp is an excellent learning tool for apprentice polymechanics to understand the logic behind ISO tolerances. Furthermore, design engineers, production planners, and ambitious hobbyists or makers benefit from professional data to implement projects with industrial precision.
